Projects

A closer look at the different types of work I create, from academic projects and client collaborations to personal experiments and pieces proudly approved by my studio assistant, Nugget.

Academic Design Studies

Projects completed through my studies at George Brown College. These pieces focus on building a strong foundation in typography, layout, and visual hierarchy while solving specific design briefs.

Focus: research, structured problem-solving, fundamentals.

Identity & Logo Explorations

A collection of logo and brand concepts created for both real and imagined clients. Each exploration looks at how a simple mark, type choice, and colour palette can define a visual voice.

Focus: logo marks, visual language, brand systems.

Client Collaborations

Freelance work for small businesses and individuals. These projects are built around real needs, clear communication, and design that supports goals such as visibility, clarity, and cohesion.

Focus: communication, briefs, practical outcomes.

Passion Projects & Experiments

Self-directed work where I test new ideas, aesthetics, and layouts without strict rules. These projects help me refine my style and explore directions I might bring into future client or school work.

Focus: experimentation, exploration, personal growth.

Studio Assistant Approved

A playful set of pieces that have passed review from Nugget, my rescue dog and unofficial creative director. He usually gets the zoomies while I am working, but his energy definitely makes the process more fun.

Focus: joy, personality, playful visual direction.
